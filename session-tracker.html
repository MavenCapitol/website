Understanding Maryland's Legislative Calendar

The Maryland General Assembly follows a consistent legislative calendar each year. Understanding these key dates is essential for planning your advocacy strategy and staying informed about pending legislation.

Session Start: The Maryland General Assembly convenes on the second Wednesday of January each year. This marks the beginning of the 90-day regular session.

Sine Die: This Latin term means "without a day" and refers to the final day of the legislative sessionâ€”typically 90 days after the session begins. After Sine Die, no more bills can be considered during that year's regular session.

Why This Matters: All bills that have not passed both chambers by Sine Die are dead for that legislative year. This creates critical deadlines for legislation you care about, making timely advocacy and awareness essential.

Planning Your Legislative Engagement

Use this session tracker to:

  • Mark Key Dates: Note when the session starts and ends so you can schedule advocacy activities.
  • Monitor Progress: Track how many days remain to advocate for or against specific bills.
  • Plan Ahead: Use the pre-session period to identify relevant bills and develop your response strategy.
  • Act Quickly: During the session, prioritize bills coming up for votes in the days remaining.
  • Understand Deadlines: Know when Sine Die approaches so you can escalate efforts for bills that haven't passed yet.
